The College Trap: What They Don't Tell You About Your Degree

Your degree is a receipt for tuition paid, not a guarantee of a career. It’s time to wake up.

Bhai, let’s talk about the **asliyat** of that expensive piece of paper you’re chasing.

You’re told to "study hard, get a degree, and life is set." **Faltu ki baat hai**. The world is changing faster than your 20-year-old curriculum.

If you think your GPA is going to save you in the age of AI, you’re in for a very rude awakening.

1. The Reality Check

College teaches you how to be a compliant employee, not a high-value executor.

You spend four years memorizing theories while a 17-year-old with a YouTube connection is building a SaaS business.

The "Coaching Culture" and the rat race have turned learning into a standardized metrics game. You aren't gaining intellect; you're gaining debt and outdated facts.

2. The "RYM" Strategy

Don't drop out, but mentally "check out" of the race. Use college for the degree (if you must), but build your **real value** outside.

The market doesn't care about your "Principles of Management" grade. It cares if you can write code, close sales, or manage AI agents.

Your goal is to become a **Self-Taught Developer** or a skilled freelancer while everyone else is busy waiting for a placement drive.

3. The 10-Minute Action Plan

  • Step 1: The Skill Audit. Look at your syllabus. Ask yourself: "How much of this will help me earn ₹1 Lakh/month next year?" If the answer is zero, you need a side-hustle.
  • Step 2: Start a Digital Project. Today. Not tomorrow. Start a blog, a small AI tool, or a portfolio. Make something that exists in the real world.
  • Step 3: Network Beyond Campus. Cold email one person who is doing what you want to do. Your network is your real degree.

Final Thoughts

College is the warm-up, but the internet is the real battlefield. **Don't show up unarmed.**
