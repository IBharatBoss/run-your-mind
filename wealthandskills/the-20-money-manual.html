The 20s Money Manual: Stop Saving, Start Investing Small

Saving money in a bank account is a guaranteed way to stay poor. Here's what the rich actually do.

You've been told since childhood to "save money for a rainy day".

You put ₹1000 in a savings account, feeling responsible. Bhai, inflation just ate your hard work.

The system wants you to be a saver because banks use your money to get rich. It's time to flip the script.

1. The Reality Check

Your savings account gives you 3-4% interest. Real inflation is easily 6-7%.

Asliyat yeh hai ki every year you leave cash in the bank, you are silently losing your purchasing power.

Rich people don't hoard cash. They hoard assets. They buy things that make more money while they sleep.

2. The "RYM" Strategy

You don't need ₹1 Lakh to start investing. You need the mindset of an investor.

Start with "Micro-Investing." The goal in your early 20s isn't to become a millionaire overnight. It's to build the habit of allocating capital.

Even ₹500 a month in a good index fund teaches you market psychology. Jugaad se paisa nahi banta, compounding se banta hai.

3. The 10-Minute Action Plan

Wealth Engine

Step 1: Open a Demat Account. Download Zerodha, Groww, or Upstox today. It takes 10 minutes and your Aadhaar card to get started.

Step 2: Start a ₹500 SIP. Pick a basic Nifty 50 Index Fund. Do not try to pick individual stocks or trade options yet. Just buy the whole market.

Step 3: Automate It. Set up a bank mandate so the money leaves your account the day after you get paid. If you don't see it, you won't spend it.

Scared of losing ₹500 in the market? You already lose that much buying junk food. Take the risk, build the wealth.
